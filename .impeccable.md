## Design Context

### Users
University of Houston students, mostly 18–24, ranging from CS majors to business students curious about cloud. They visit the site to decide whether to join, find out about events, or get a feel for the community. Context: browsing late at night after class, from a dorm room, campus library, or apartment. They are skeptical of corporate polish and respond to authenticity. They want to feel like they found their people — not like they landed on a vendor page.

### Brand Personality
**scrappy · technical · community-first**

Like boot.dev: confident, direct, built by people who actually use the tools. The tone is peer-to-peer, never top-down. No "we are pleased to announce" energy. This club earns credibility through clarity and realness, not marketing gloss.

Emotional goals: visitors should feel *recognized* ("this was made for people like me") and *motivated* ("I could learn here").

### Aesthetic Direction
**Dark techy — earned, not performed.** Dark because this is software people use at night while learning, not because dark looks cool. The design should feel like it came from someone with good taste who also writes CloudFormation templates.

**References:**
- boot.dev — clarity, function-first, developer credibility without flexing
- Raycast — precision spacing, confident typography, no wasted pixels  
- genesisblockchain.io — architectural restraint, dark done seriously
- basistheory.com — modern structure without corporate stiffness

**Anti-references:**
- aws.amazon.com community pages — corporate, stiff, vendor-y
- Generic university club pages — SharePoint vibes, stock photos, forgettable
- AI slop web pages — gradient text on every heading, aurora blobs, identical glass cards, cyan-purple glow everywhere, stats bars with big numbers

**Theme:** Dark. Students use this at night. Dark is the right choice here, not a lazy default.

**Colors:** Purple/cyan palette — but executed with OKLCH for perceptual consistency and tinted neutrals. Purple is the primary accent (violet family, ~290° hue). Cyan as secondary, used very sparingly. Neutrals tinted toward purple so the whole surface feels cohesive. Accent appears rarely — it should mean something when it does.

**Typography:**
- Display/headings: **Bricolage Grotesque** — compressed feel at heavy weights, editorial grit, not corporate, not tryhard
- Body: **Figtree** — clean, slightly rounded, readable, peer-to-peer energy

### Design Principles
1. **Earned confidence** — The site should look like it was built by someone who knows what they're doing, not by a marketing team. Simplicity that's been thought through beats decoration that hasn't.
2. **Accent is a signal, not decoration** — Purple/cyan used at most in 10% of the visual weight. When the accent color appears, it means something. Overuse kills it.
3. **Left-aligned, structured, asymmetric** — Not everything centered. Type hierarchy and layout do the work that effects are tempted to do.
4. **Community over institution** — Copy and layout feel human and direct. Peer-to-peer tone throughout.
5. **No AI tells** — No gradient text, no identical card grids, no stats bars, no aurora blobs, no glassmorphism everywhere, no side-stripe borders.
